Description

The Labrador Retriever, or just Labrador, is a large breed of retriever-gun dog. It is one of the most popular dog breeds in Canada, the UK, and the US. Labradors are often used as assistance dogs and in detection roles by official agencies.

Origins

The modern Labrador's ancestors originated in Newfoundland, Canada. The breed descended from the St. John's water dog, mixed with mastiffs brought by Portuguese fishermen. These smaller dogs became the forebears of the Labrador Retriever.

Temperament

Labradors are known for their even temperament and compatibility with children and other animals. They are energetic and athletic, often needing early training to manage their enthusiasm.

Health

Labradors are generally healthy but may face issues like inherited disorders and obesity. Studies have shown that Chocolate Labradors may have a shorter life expectancy and more health problems due to selective breeding.

Appearance Around the World

Labradors became more popular in the US post-WWII, gaining popularity for their versatility in hunting and companionship. In Russia and the former USSR, their presence grew in the late 1960s.
